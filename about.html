K Abhuday / ZAGRAYWOLF

the human who runs this alley.

Hey, I'm Kunwar Abhuday Singh. I'm happiest at the edge of something I don't understand yet, and most of what I know came from chasing that feeling.

It started with wanting to make a machine see. Teaching a robot to figure out where it was in the world showed me that the gap between a clean idea and something that holds up at twenty frames a second is where all the real learning hides. That hooked me, and the questions only got bigger from there.

Chasing the models behind the vision taught me how deep learning actually thinks. Squeezing those models onto tiny hardware taught me that constraints make you honest about what a system really needs. Getting software to act on its own taught me how much care it takes to make something autonomous you'd actually trust. And keeping all of it running once it left my laptop taught me the unglamorous engineering that turns a demo into something real.

Every one of those was something I had no clue how to do when I started, and that's the part I love. I learn fastest by jumping into a problem that's a little over my head, digging until the abstraction gives way and I can see the gears, and coming out knowing something I didn't before. Curiosity does most of the driving; stubbornness handles the rest.

So I'm less a list of titles and more someone still collecting skills, still genuinely excited by a new tool or idea, and always up for the next thing I haven't figured out yet. If you're building something hard and interesting, I'd love to learn it with you.

I built this site by hand, no framework, no template, to make a corner of the web that feels like mine. It's a Tokyo alley after dark: my work and projects are manga chapters you flip through, a stall out back serves the anime, films, and books I love, and a small white cat named Casper supervises. Pull up a stool. Stay a while.

DESIGN PHILOSOPHY

A portfolio shouldn't read like a form. This one's a place you walk through: a moody street with signs you light up by getting close, a skyline that drifts as you move, glyphs that scatter from your cursor. It's built progressively, so it works with JavaScript off, respects reduced motion, and is tuned for reading at night.

COLOPHON

Built by hand. No framework, no build step, no template. Just vanilla HTML, CSS, and ES module JavaScript; Canvas 2D for the curator, the helix, and the game; native Web Audio for the lo-fi. It works with JavaScript off and respects reduced motion. One cat. It keeps anonymous visit analytics (no cookies) and honors Do-Not-Track.

That white blur following your cursor is Casper. Pet him enough and he gets ideas, or just hit the arcade cabinet in the corner and play.
